Camelot is Bad

TL;DR: Think twice before renting a property from or associated with Camelot. They severely disadvantage tenants and systematically seek the boundaries of the tenancy law.

In recent years Camelot has been regularly discredited by serious malpractices in their business operations of renting out properties to - often vulnerable - tenants. Problems range from not paying back deposits after the termination of a lease, charging wrongful service costs to poor or no maintenance of properties.

This overview serves as a warning for people who intend to rent a property from Camelot. As a former tenant, I strongly advise you not to rent a property from them. They take advantage of the scarcity on the housing market to charge inappropriate high costs.

Examples of Camelot wrongfully charging costs

  • Tenants Rijswijkstraat (Amsterdam) are eligible for a refund for overcharged service costs in 2015 (~500 euro), 2016 (~580 euro) and 2018 (500 euro or more).
  • Camelot charged various start-up costs of 202.50 euros and cleaning costs of 100 euros. The judge ruled that tenants are eligible for a full refund.

Now in court

  • Since 2014 a case is running of 76 tenants Rijswijkstraat (Amsterdam) against Clear Real Estate for excessive rents. Several judges ruled in favour of the tenants. A final verdict on the WOZ-value is awaited. The current verdict ruled that tenants are eligible for a refund of ~240 euro per month, with retroactive effect until 2014.
