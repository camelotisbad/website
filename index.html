Camelot is Bad

TL;DR: Think twice before renting a property from or associated with Camelot. They severely disadvantage tenants and systematically seek the boundaries of the tenancy law.

Legend Examples, Running cases, What should I do?, News

In recent years, Camelot has been exposed several times for serious malpractices in their business operations of renting out properties to - often vulnerable - tenants. Problems range from not paying back deposits after the termination of a lease, charging wrongful service costs to poor or no maintenance of properties.

This overview serves as a warning for people who intend to rent a property from Camelot. As a former tenant, I strongly advise you to reconsider renting a property from them or a company associated with Camelot. This warning also applies to Clear Real Estate (49% owned by Camelot), B-Right Urban Living (same owners as Camelot) and Mosaic World (from January, 1st 2021 the new name of Camelot Europe).

Examples of Camelot wrongfully charging costs

  • Tenants Rijswijkstraat (Amsterdam) are eligible for a refund for overcharged service costs in 2015 (~500 euro), 2016 (~580 euro) and 2018 (500 euro or more).
  • Camelot charged various start-up costs of 202.50 euros and cleaning costs of 100 euros. The judge ruled that tenants are eligible for a full refund.

Now in court

  • Since 2014 a case is running of 76 tenants Rijswijkstraat (Amsterdam) against Clear Real Estate for excessive rents. Several judges ruled in favour of the tenants. A final verdict on the WOZ-value is awaited. The current verdict ruled that tenants are eligible for a refund of ~240 euro per month, with retroactive effect until 2014. The total claim may amount to around 1 million euro.

What should I do?

  1. Do a rent check when you live in social housing and think the rent it to high. On the basis of a score, a maximum rental price is calculated.
  2. Ask for advice from !WOON or any other tenant information and advice point. They can help you take the correct next steps.
  3. Check the annual statement of service costs for unexplained or incorrect costs.
  4. Start a procedure with the rental commission when you are sure that the rent or service costs are too high.
